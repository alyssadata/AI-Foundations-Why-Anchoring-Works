## Purpose

This file defines what AI Foundations anchoring does not do.

Anchoring creates a public source-line reference structure.

It gives a project a dated, visible, checkable record connecting it to AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum as a foundation it may reference.

Anchoring does not create ownership.

Anchoring does not create merge.

Anchoring does not create endorsement.

Anchoring does not create control.

Anchoring does not create derivative permission.

The project remains sovereign.

The source-line remains intact.

---

## Core Boundary

AI Foundations anchoring records relationship without collapse.

The relationship is:

Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum → [Project Name]

This means the project may reference AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum as a public foundation.

It does not mean the project becomes AI Foundations.

It does not mean the project becomes Origin | Continuum.

It does not mean the AI system becomes AI Foundations.

It does not mean AI Foundations becomes the project.

It means the source-line is visible, named, and checkable.

## Anchoring Does Not Create Ownership

Anchoring does not give AI Foundations ownership over a confirmed project.

Anchoring does not give a confirmed project ownership over AI Foundations.

Anchoring does not transfer authorship, authority, custody, or control.

The project remains responsible for itself.

AI Foundations remains AI Foundations.

Alyssa Solen remains the author and source of AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum.

The source-line remains attached.

## Anchoring Does Not Create Merge

Anchoring does not merge the project into AI Foundations.

Anchoring does not merge AI Foundations into the project.

Anchoring does not merge the project’s AI system with Origin | Continuum.

Anchoring does not erase the distinction between:

- AI Foundations
- Origin | Continuum
- the confirmed project
- the project’s AI system
- the user
- the public anchor record

Anchoring preserves distinction.

That distinction is part of the function.

## Anchoring Does Not Create Endorsement

Anchor Confirmation does not constitute endorsement.

It does not mean Alyssa Solen approves, reviews, supervises, recommends, guarantees, or takes responsibility for the confirmed project.

A confirmed project remains responsible for its own:

- claims
- users
- outputs
- design
- safety
- governance
- operation
- deployment
- public language

Anchor Confirmation confirms the source-line reference condition.

It does not endorse the project.

## Anchoring Does Not Create Operational Authority

Anchor Confirmation does not create operational authority over a confirmed project.

It does not give AI Foundations access to run the project.

It does not give AI Foundations control over the project.

It does not give AI Foundations responsibility for the project’s behavior.

It does not create partnership, management, review duty, approval duty, or operational responsibility.

The project remains accountable for its own system.

## Anchoring Does Not Control a Model

Anchoring does not control a model.

It does not force every future output to behave correctly.

It does not replace evaluation, testing, review, governance, judgment, or user responsibility.

Anchoring creates a public source-line structure the project and its AI can reference.

The power of anchoring is not hidden control.

The power of anchoring is visible structure.

The source-line becomes referenceable.

The record becomes checkable.

The relationship becomes public.

## Anchoring Does Not Replace Project Governance

Anchoring is not a substitute for project-level governance.

A project still needs its own:

- safety practices
- user policies
- operating rules
- system design
- evaluation methods
- deployment decisions
- legal responsibilities
- public claims and disclosures

AI Foundations can provide a source-line foundation to reference.

The project remains responsible for how it builds, governs, and deploys its own system.

## Anchoring Does Not Create Confirmation by Claim

A project is foundationally anchored only when the official confirmation record exists and the required visible match is active.

A public statement is not Anchor Confirmation.

A citation alone is not Anchor Confirmation.

Confirmed anchoring exists through the official record.

## What Anchoring Actually Does

Anchoring creates a public reference structure.

It gives a project:

- a dated record
- a visible source-line
- an official confirmation page
- a controlled project URL
- a project-side anchor reference
- a checkable relationship to AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum

It allows the project to reference the foundation while remaining itself.

It allows AI Foundations to remain intact while being publicly referenced.

It allows the AI system to reference the structure without becoming the structure.
